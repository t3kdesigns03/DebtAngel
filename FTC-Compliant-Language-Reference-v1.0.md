# Debt Angel Compliant Language Reference
**Version 1.0 — July 2026**

## Core Positioning (Use This)

**Primary message:**
See your current path versus a structured debt resolution plan — side by side — before you decide.

**Supporting messages:**
- Compare your estimated monthly deposit, total cost, and timeline with continuing minimum payments.
- You stay in control. You approve every settlement.
- Designed for qualifying unsecured debt only.
- No upfront fees. Performance-based pricing.

**Avoid:**
- "Least amount of risk"
- "Smarter. Faster. Cheaper."
- Any implication that Debt Angel reduces risk compared to other options

## 1. Savings & Cost Language

**Approved framing:**
- "Estimated difference versus minimum payments"
- "Estimated all-in cost with Debt Angel"
- "What you could pay if you continue minimum payments"

**Example:**
Estimated difference versus minimum payments: $X,XXX — Estimated all-in program cost: $X,XXX

**Avoid:**
- "Projected savings"
- "You could save $XXX,XXX"
- Any language that implies Debt Angel creates savings rather than showing a comparison

**Key rule:** Always frame numbers as a comparison between two paths, not as money Debt Angel will save the user.

## 2. Credit Impact Language (Must Be Direct)

**Approved phrasing:**
- "Debt resolution may negatively affect your credit during the program."
- "Credit recovery is possible after accounts are resolved, but timing and results vary by individual."
- "Credit outcomes are not guaranteed."

**When discussing recovery:**
After accounts are resolved, Debt Angel provides general educational guidance to help you rebuild credit. Results vary significantly based on your individual situation.

**Avoid:**
- "Faster credit recovery"
- "Credit usually recovers faster"
- Specific score examples in marketing (e.g., "628 → 704")
- Any implication that credit improvement is a primary or expected outcome

## 3. What Debt Angel Is / Is Not

**Approved section header:** What Debt Angel is designed for — and what it is not

**Approved language:**
- Debt Angel is a debt resolution program for qualifying unsecured debt.
- Debt Angel is not bankruptcy.
- Debt Angel is not a foreclosure prevention or mortgage modification program.
- Debt Angel is not a repossession prevention program.
- Debt Angel is not a short-sale service.
- Debt Angel does not work with secured debts such as mortgages or auto loans in most cases.

**Avoid:**
- "No foreclosure. Your home stays your home."
- "No repossession. Keep the car in the driveway."
- Any language that implies protection of homes, cars, or other secured assets.

## 4. Estimator Framing & Assumptions

**Primary result framing:**
Estimated difference versus minimum payments: $X,XXX

**Visible assumptions (show near results):**
This estimate assumes: continued minimum payments on your current path, no new charges, estimated APR, creditor participation, consistent monthly deposits into the program, and typical settlement ranges. Actual results vary.

**Additional required context:**
Debt resolution may affect your credit. Forgiven debt may be taxable. This is an illustration, not a guarantee or offer.

Avoid showing large savings numbers without the comparison context and assumptions nearby.

## 5. Testimonials & Example Scenarios

**Approved section title:** Example scenarios

**Required labeling:**
These are illustrative examples based on composite client experiences. Individual results vary based on your specific debts, creditors, ability to fund a plan, and other factors.

**Approved example format:**
Example: $38,400 in qualifying unsecured debt resolved through a structured plan over 26 months.

**Avoid:**
- Specific outcomes that sound like guarantees (especially credit score jumps)
- Any mention of keeping homes or cars in testimonials
- Presenting composites as "real client stories" without clear illustrative labeling

## 6. FAQ — Required Direct Answers
These answers should be visible on the page (not just expandable questions).

**Will this affect my credit?** Yes, it may. Debt settlement programs can negatively impact your credit score during the time you are enrolled. After accounts are resolved, many people work to rebuild their credit, but results vary.

**Is forgiven debt taxable?** It can be. The IRS generally treats canceled or forgiven debt as taxable income unless an exception or exclusion applies. You should consult a tax professional regarding your specific situation.

**What kinds of debt qualify?** Debt Angel is designed for qualifying unsecured debts, such as credit cards, personal loans, and certain medical bills. Secured debts (mortgages, auto loans, etc.) and some other types of debt typically do not qualify. Some debts, creditors, and states may not be eligible.

Other key answers should be direct, avoid hype, and clearly state limitations and variability.

## 7. General Rules & Forbidden Phrases

**Forbidden / High-Risk Phrases (Do Not Use):**
- "Least amount of risk"
- "Your Debt Zero with the least amount of risk"
- "No foreclosure / Your home stays your home"
- "No repossession / Keep the car in the driveway"
- "Faster credit recovery"
- "Credit started climbing back faster"
- "Projected savings" (when used as the primary framing)
- Any guarantee of specific timelines, savings percentages, or outcomes

**Tone Guidelines:**
- Be direct and honest rather than reassuring.
- Emphasize user control and transparency ("You approve every step").
- Clearly separate what Debt Angel does from what it does not do.
- When in doubt, be more conservative.

## 8. Required Disclaimers (Use Consistently)
Place a clear version of this near major claims and the estimator:

Debt Angel is not a lender, credit repair organization, credit counseling agency, or law firm. We do not provide legal or tax advice. Programs are not available in all states. Results are not guaranteed and vary based on individual circumstances, creditor participation, and ability to fund a plan. Debt resolution may negatively affect your credit. Forgiven debt may be taxable. This content is for informational purposes only.
